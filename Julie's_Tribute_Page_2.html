The branches of philosophy do not change. These are the same branches that existed in minds during the time of Aristotle. People think about our existence as human beings in four ways:

As we begin our journey, it is helpful to get a quick overview of the questions we will try to answer.

Our list of questions about philosophy

  • What is a philosophy?
  • What are the branches of philosophy?
  • What is a paradigm?
  • What are the different paradigms associated with education?
  • What are the different paradigms associated with psychology?
  • What is a learning theory and how does it fit into philosophy?
  • What is a conceptual framework?
  • What does a teaching philosophy look like?

In addition, when we are greeted with ten paradigms most suited for inquiry in the world of teaching, we will target particular topics and questions so we can then compare and contrast each paradigm. This close study will be beneficial as we move forward and begin writing our teaching philosophy

  1. What are some paradigms in education while others are in psychology?
  2. If I had to capture a big idea to represent each paradigm what would they be
  3. What content is central in each paradigm and who decides what it studied?
  4. What is the expected role of a teacher?
  5. What is the expected role of the learner?
  6. What learning environment is supported by this paradigm?
  7. How does this paradigm view learning in the brain?
    • How are the views of education paradigms similar to those from psychology?
    • How are the views of education paradigms different from psychology?
  8. What learning theories/theorists seem to reside within each paradigm?
  9. How do I decide how to align my own belief system?
